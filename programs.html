Our Services & Programs

Our work is focused on eight key service areas designed to combat social exclusion, inequality, and discrimination against marginalized groups in Ghana.

Eight Pillars of Inclusion

Advocacy & Awareness

Challenging stigma and pushing for inclusive policy changes.

Empowerment & Capacity

Building skills for self-advocacy and economic independence.

Public Consultations

Engaging stakeholders for inclusive community planning.

Rehabilitation & Support

Providing physical, vocational, and psychosocial rehabilitation.

Assistive Technology

Facilitating access to essential equipment and tech solutions.

Accessibility Audits

Evaluating and advising on accessible infrastructure and services.

Inclusive Recreation

Organizing adaptive sports and cultural participation programs.

Disability Research

Gathering data to inform policy and drive evidence-based action.

In-Depth Look at Our Services

1. Advocacy and Awareness

We drive public campaigns and media outreach to change negative perceptions and challenge deep-rooted stigma against marginalized groups. Our advocacy focuses on influencing government and corporate policy to ensure the rights enshrined in the Disability Act are fully implemented.

2. Empowerment & Capacity Building

This involves training sessions focused on self-advocacy, leadership, and vocational skills. We aim to equip individuals with the knowledge and confidence necessary to participate actively in community and economic life, turning beneficiaries into self-reliant change agents.

3. Public Consultations

We facilitate dialogue between marginalized groups, local authorities, policymakers, and community leaders. These consultations ensure that development plans, government policies, and service delivery mechanisms are informed by the lived experiences of those they are intended to serve.

4. Rehabilitation & Support Services

We provide direct support including physical therapy, psychosocial counseling, educational integration support, and vocational guidance to help individuals maximize their physical, sensory, intellectual, psychological, and social functioning.

5. Assistive Technology & Equipment

This program focuses on identifying, sourcing, and distributing essential aids such as wheelchairs, white canes, prosthetic devices, and digital tools that enhance communication and learning, thereby removing critical barriers to participation.

6. Public Accessibility Audits

We conduct comprehensive assessments of public buildings, transportation systems, and digital platforms against national and international accessibility standards. We then work with owners and operators to implement necessary modifications.

7. Inclusive Recreation Programs

We organize sports, arts, and cultural activities tailored to ensure full participation by all members of the community, regardless of ability. This promotes social integration, teamwork, and physical well-being.

8. Disability Research

Our research initiatives collect reliable local data on the scale of exclusion, specific barriers faced, and the effectiveness of current interventions. This evidence is crucial for guiding our programmatic strategy and influencing evidence-based policy making at the national level.
